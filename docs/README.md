## Purpose

This README serves as the central hub for discovering and navigating all project management process documentation at OctoAcme. Whether you're a new team member getting oriented, a project manager looking for templates, or a developer seeking clarity on our delivery practices, this guide will help you quickly find the information you need.

Our goal is to:
- **Improve discoverability** of project management processes across the organization
- **Support onboarding** of new team members with clear, accessible process documentation
- **Enable easier navigation** across interconnected process docs
- **Provide a quick reference** for project management practices and key artifacts

## OctoAcme Project Management Approach

OctoAcme follows a structured yet lightweight five-phase project lifecycle that balances predictability with agility: **Initiation → Planning → Execution → Release → Retrospective**. During **Initiation**, we validate the business need, align stakeholders, and create a Project One-pager that defines the problem, goals, and success metrics. The **Planning** phase transforms that approved initiative into an actionable backlog with clear acceptance criteria, timelines, and a Definition of Done. Throughout **Execution**, we maintain a steady rhythm of daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and regular demos, while tracking progress on project boards and managing risks proactively. When ready, we move into the **Release** phase with pre-deployment checklists, smoke tests, and rollback plans to ensure safe production deployments. Finally, each project or sprint concludes with a **Retrospective** where we capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements.

Our processes emphasize lightweight artifacts over heavy documentation. Key deliverables include the Project One-pager (problem statement and success metrics), a prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria, a Risk Register for tracking and mitigating issues, and regular status reports that keep stakeholders informed. We avoid unnecessary ceremony while ensuring critical information is captured and accessible to the team.

Core roles drive accountability and collaboration across projects. The **Project Manager** coordinates delivery activities, manages schedules and risks, and facilitates communication across teams. The **Product Manager** defines what should be built, prioritizes the roadmap based on customer value, and measures outcomes against success metrics. **Developers** implement features, write tests, and participate in design and code reviews. **QA/Testing** validates that acceptance criteria are met and ensures quality standards. Together, these roles create a customer-first, iterative delivery culture.

Communication and quality are foundational to our approach. Teams hold daily standups to surface blockers and dependencies, while weekly delivery syncs provide visibility into progress and flagged risks. Stakeholder updates occur monthly or at key milestones, and demos showcase working software regularly. Quality assurance is built into every stage: developers write unit tests and integration tests, CI/CD pipelines run automated checks and security scans, pull requests require peer review, and smoke tests verify critical flows before release. Retrospectives close the loop by ensuring we continuously learn and improve from every project, turning insights into action items that make the next cycle even better.

## Getting Started

1. **New to OctoAcme?** Start with the [Project Management Overview](octoacme-project-management-overview.md) to understand our principles and lifecycle.

2. **Starting a new project?** Follow the [Project Initiation Guide](octoacme-project-initiation.md) to create a One-pager and align stakeholders.

3. **Managing an active project?** Refer to [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) for day-to-day workflows and [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md) for keeping stakeholders informed.

4. **Preparing for a release?** Use the [Release & Deployment Guide](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) to ensure a smooth, safe deployment.

5. **Want to improve?** Run a [Retrospective](octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md) to capture learnings and action items.
